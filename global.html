Common Global Cyber Attacks

  • Ransomware: A type of malware that encrypts files and demands a ransom for their decryption. Notable examples: WannaCry, NotPetya.
  • Phishing Attacks: Fraudulent communications designed to trick individuals into revealing sensitive information. Often seen in emails, websites, or messages.
  • Denial of Service (DoS) Attacks: Attackers overwhelm a network or server with traffic, making it inaccessible. A distributed variant (DDoS) amplifies the effect.
  • SQL Injection: Attackers insert malicious SQL code into a website's database, gaining unauthorized access to sensitive data.
  • Man-in-the-Middle (MitM) Attacks: Interception of communication between two parties to steal data or inject malicious content.
  • Malware: Software specifically designed to harm or exploit devices. Examples include viruses, worms, trojans, and spyware.

  • Insider Threats: Employees or contractors misuse their access to company data or systems to compromise security.

How to Protect Yourself

Protecting yourself from global cyber threats requires a combination of awareness and action. Here are a few key strategies:

  • Regularly update your software and security patches to fix vulnerabilities.
  • Use strong passwords and enable multi-factor authentication wherever possible.
  • Be cautious when clicking on links or downloading files, especially from unknown sources.
  • Backup important data frequently to avoid data loss in case of ransomware attacks.
  • Secure your network by using a VPN and strong Wi-Fi encryption (WPA3).
